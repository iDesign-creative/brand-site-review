About iDesign

Learning should be shaped with intention.

That takes collaboration with educators, with organizations, with the people closest to the work.
And it stays rooted in the reason teaching matters in the first place: the learner.

‍Our founders spent years in the first wave of online program management and saw a fundamental mismatch: instructional design isn't one-size-fits-all, but the industry was selling it that way. Programs got locked into rigid contracts that couldn't flex with what they actually needed. In 2013, Paxton Riter, Whitney Kilgore, and Ned Stone started iDesign on a different premise: services shaped around each partner, with the right support for the work in front of them.

Organizations deserved a true partner
Educators deserved hands-on support
Online programs should be built around learners, not revenue-share contracts

Our partnerships extend far beyond course design. We come in as strategic partners, working alongside organizations to rethink their teaching approach, refine their program structure, and prepare for what's next in learning.

Tim Hamelen

Chief Marketing Officer

Tim leads marketing at iDesign, both for the company and for the online programs it builds with its partners. He connects what each institution offers with the learners it needs to reach, drawing on more than two decades in performance and growth marketing. Before iDesign, he held marketing leadership roles at Academic Partnerships, Science Mobile, and Postie, spanning higher education and technology.

Sam Foster

Chief Financial Officer

Sam serves as Chief Financial Officer of iDesign, where he leads the company's financial strategy, FP&A, accounting, treasury, and banking relationships. His focus on profitability and long-term decision-making helps keep iDesign a steady partner to the institutions it serves. Since joining the company, he has built and scaled its finance and accounting functions as the business has grown. He holds degrees from the University of Richmond and the McCombs School of Business at the University of Texas at Austin.

Whitney Kilgore

Co-founder & CAO

Whitney has spent her career at the intersection of teaching, learning, and technology. She collaborates with academic teams to design courses and programs that are grounded in strong pedagogy. Before iDesign, she led instructional design and learning innovation efforts at multiple institutions and education partners. She holds a PhD in Learning Technologies from the University of North Texas and has worked in both K-12 and higher education.

Paxton Riter

Co-Founder & CEO

Paxton co-founded iDesign with a clear goal: help institutions build flexible, effective learning experiences by working alongside them as true partners. He leads the company’s direction and growth, drawing on years of experience in education and business. Before starting iDesign, he held leadership roles at Academic Partnerships and Best Associates, supporting universities in launching online programs around the world. He holds degrees from Washington and Lee University and the Cox School of Business at SMU.

Ned Stone

Co-Founder & COO

Ned leads the company’s operations, connecting long-term strategy to the day-to-day work that drives results. He holds a degree from Washington and Lee University and completed doctoral work at Oregon State University. Before co-founding iDesign, Ned taught economics at Oregon State and contributed to federal research focused on land use and environmental policy. His background in systems thinking and applied research influences how he supports teams.

Thomas B. Evans

Board Member

Thomas B. Evans is a seasoned education entrepreneur and investor with a long track record of founding and scaling impactful ventures in higher education. As Founder and Managing Partner of Skyline Merchant Partners, a Dallas-based firm focused on high-growth education businesses, Evans has helped drive innovation and access across the sector.

Dr. Vernon Johnson

Board Member

Dr. Vernon Johnson brings over three decades of leadership in both K-12 public education and edtech innovation. A former superintendent in Texas and Minnesota, he was twice selected as one of America’s Top 100 School Executives by the American Association of School Administrators.

Amelia Manning

Board Member

Amelia Manning is a distinguished leader in online and adult education, known for her transformative leadership and student-centric innovation. She is widely recognized as a trailblazer in data-driven education strategy and a champion for expanding access and success for working adult learners.
